Michael Jordan Career Timeline

July 19, 1984

Jordan was drafted 3rd overall in the 1984 drafted by the Chicago Bulls being selected after Sam Bowie via the Portland TrailBlazers and fellow Hall of Famer Hakeem Olajuwon via the Houston Rockets.

October 26, 1984

Jordan made his NBA debut in Chicago against the Washington Bullets. He had a stateline on 16 points, 7 assist, 6 rebounds, 4 blocks and 2 steals all in 40 minutes of play.

May 17, 1985

Michael Jordan was named the rookie of the year for the 1984-85 season. He won the award over number 1 pick Hakeem Olajuwan. Jordan averaged 28 points per game led the NBA in total points and was 4th in steals averaging 2.39 a game.

April 20, 1986

Jordan scored 63 points in a playoff game against the Boston Celtics in a losing effort. Boston would eventually end up winning the finals that year against Houston.

June 12, 1991

Michael Jordan won his 1st NBA title against Magic Johnson and the Los Angeles Lakers. The Bulls defeated the defending champions Detroit Pistons AKA the "Bad Boy Pistons" in the conference Finals after struggling against them in previous years.

June 14, 1992

Jordan won his 2nd NBA Championship against the Portland TrailBlazers.

1992 Olympics

Jordan played on the 1992 United States mens basketball team. They were so talented that they were nicknamed the Dream Team. They won the gold medal after beating Croatia in Barcelona.

June 21, 1993

Michael Jordan accomplished a 3Peat winning his 3rd championship in a row against the Charles Barkley led Phoenix Suns.

October 6, 1993

Michael Jordan shortly after winning his 3rd championship and the death of his father decided to retire. He chose to tryout baseball and played for the Chicago White Sox minor league team.

March 18, 1995

After a full year Jordan announced his return to the NBA. He came back wearing the number 45 with his first game being against Reggie Miller and the Indiana Pacers.

June 16, 1996

Jordan would end up his 1st championship since returning to the NBA and his 4th overall against Gary Payton Sean Kempt and the Seattle SuperSonics.

June 13, 1997

Jordan won his 5th ring against John Stockton Karl Malone and the Utah Jazz. This series is especially known for the famous "Flu Game" in which Jordan played while sick and still scoring 38 points in a Bulls victory to take the series lead and eventually end up winning the series.

June 14, 1998

Jordan won his 6th championship in a rematch with the Utah Jazz. This would also be his last game with the Bulls as he would retire a 2nd time. It was also the end of the Chicago Bulls dynasty. They have not won or even been in the Finals since Jordan left.

October 30, 2001

Jordan would come out of retirement again at the age of 38 and play with the Washington Wizards. His first game was against the New York Knicks.

December 28, 2001

Jordan dropped 50 points with the Wizards against the Charlotte Hornets.

April 16, 2003

Michael Jordan would play his last ever NBA game and retire for good. his last game was played in Philly in a lost against Allen Iverson and the Philadelphia 76ers.

September 11, 2009

Michael Jordan was inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame.
